## 2. FEATURE HIGHLIGHTS

### Feature 1: The 10-Point Slop Checklist
The skill runs every piece of text through a mandatory 10-point inspection. Structural patterns, word choice, rhythm, sentence openers, paragraph flow. Nothing gets a free pass. This is not a vibe check. It is a systematic teardown.

**Example it catches:** "It's worth noting that in today's rapidly evolving landscape, organizations must navigate..." -- flagged for throat-clearing opener, banned words ("landscape," "navigate," "it's worth noting"), and zero information density.

---

### Feature 2: The Slop Scale (0-10 Scoring)
Your text gets a concrete score. Zero means it reads like a human wrote it on purpose. Ten means it could have been generated by prompting "write me a LinkedIn post." You see the number, you see what dragged it up, and you see how to bring it down.

**Example it catches:** A paragraph scoring 7/10 because it opens with a rhetorical question, uses three em-dashes in two sentences, and contains "delve," "foster," and "harness" within 40 words.

---

### Feature 3: Bilingual Pattern Detection (EN/DE)
AI slop does not stop at English. German AI output has its own tells -- "im Bereich von," "zueglich," "nicht zuletzt," "in der heutigen Zeit." The skill carries dedicated banned-word lists for both languages and detects language-specific structural patterns.

**Example it catches:** "In der heutigen digitalen Welt ist es wichtiger denn je, innovative Loesungen zu entwickeln, die einen echten Mehrwert bieten." -- flagged for German AI boilerplate opener, buzzword stacking ("innovative Loesungen," "echten Mehrwert"), and empty framing.

---

### Feature 4: Structural Pattern Detection
AI text has a skeleton you can spot from a mile away. It opens with a rhetorical question. Every paragraph gets a three-item list. Em-dashes substitute for actual punctuation. Passive constructions dodge accountability. This skill identifies those bones and breaks them.

**Example it catches:** "What does this mean for the future of work? Well, three things: flexibility, innovation, and resilience. These pillars -- each critical in its own right -- form the foundation of..." -- flagged for rhetorical question opener, triple-list fetish, em-dash inflation, and "pillar/foundation" metaphor recycling.

---

### Feature 5: Human Rewriting, Not Just Red Ink
Flagging problems is the easy part. This skill goes further: it rewrites flagged passages so they sound like someone with actual expertise sat down and wrote them. Shorter sentences where needed. Specific claims instead of vague gestures. Active voice. A point of view.

**Example it catches and fixes:** "This groundbreaking solution leverages cutting-edge technology to empower teams and drive meaningful results" becomes "The tool automates three steps your team currently does by hand, cutting turnaround from five days to one."

## 4. FAQ SECTION

### Is this free?
Yes. Completely free. No premium tier, no usage limits, no "free for 7 days" nonsense. It is an open-source Claude Code skill.

### Do I need Claude Code?
Yes. This is a custom skill built for Claude Code, Anthropic's CLI-based coding tool. You install it as a skill, and it runs inside your Claude Code sessions. If you do not have Claude Code, you will need to set that up first.

### Does it work with German?
Yes. It carries a dedicated German banned-word list and detects German-specific AI patterns: the "heutige digitale Welt" openers, the "ganzheitlich/massgeschneidert/Mehrwert" trifecta, the reflexive "es ermoeglicht" hedging, and the passive constructions that German AI output loves. It scores and rewrites German text the same way it handles English.

### Will it change my meaning?
No. The skill rewrites for clarity and human tone, not for different content. If your original text says "we reduced costs by 30%," the rewrite keeps that claim. What changes is the packaging: the skill strips the filler, tightens the structure, and stops the wording from performing.

### How is this different from Grammarly?
Grammarly checks grammar, spelling, and basic style. It will tell you a sentence is passive or too long. It will not tell you that your opening paragraph reads like it was generated by an LLM because it uses "delve," "landscape," and a rhetorical question in the first three lines. This skill specifically targets AI writing patterns: the structural habits, word choices, and rhythmic tells that make text sound machine-generated. Grammarly polishes. This skill de-robots.

### What is the "Slop Scale"?
A 0-to-10 score measuring how AI-generated your text sounds. Zero means it reads like a human wrote it. Ten means an AI clearly generated it and nobody edited. The score is based on banned-word density, structural pattern frequency, sentence rhythm variety, and information-to-filler ratio. It is not a quality score. It specifically measures AI tells.

### Can I customize the banned words list?
Yes. The skill uses a configurable list. If your industry has legitimate reasons to use words like "leverage" or "synergy" (it probably does not, but we will give you the benefit of the doubt), you can adjust the list.

### Does it work on long documents?
Yes. Feed it a full blog post, a white paper section, or a marketing page. It processes the entire text, scores it, and rewrites the flagged sections. For very long documents, running it section by section tends to produce better results.
